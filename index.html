Circadian Shelf

I like collecting small fragments of daily moments because they don’t ask to be important. They’re usually the parts of a day that pass unnoticed—light, pauses, surfaces, reflections. Holding onto these fragments feels calmer than trying to capture whole experiences.

I don’t collect events. I collect the parts that almost disappear—then decide how long to hold them.

How I keep things

  • glance brief, no words
  • held annotated (light/body)
  • kept a reason why
  • revisited updated later

Text fragments are what I couldn’t photograph.

  • Condition: Mutable
